# Shopfront: Product Pitch + Customer User Guide

## Product Pitch

### What Shopfront Is
Shopfront helps small businesses launch and maintain a professional website by text message.

No dashboard to learn. No web editor to manage. No need to hire a developer for every update.

You text your business details to Shopfront, and your website is generated automatically with:
- Business name and category
- Services/menu with prices
- Hours
- Address and contact actions
- Notices (closures, promotions, announcements)
- Photos (banner/gallery)

### Core Value Add

#### 1. Fastest path from “no website” to live page
Most owners can go live in minutes through a guided onboarding conversation.

#### 2. Updates happen the way owners already work
Owners text what changed. Shopfront interprets it, confirms the update, then publishes the change.

#### 3. Built for real small-business operations
When prices, hours, or temporary closures change, the site can be updated immediately without logging into complex tools.

#### 4. Lower overhead, fewer tools
One conversational channel replaces website editor workflows for common updates.

#### 5. Human backup when needed
Support starts with AI for speed, then escalates to a human when required.

## How Customers Use Shopfront

## Part A: New Business Sign-Up and Website Launch

### Step 1: Start a conversation
The owner sends a first message to the Shopfront number (SMS or WhatsApp).

### Step 2: Complete guided onboarding
Shopfront asks for key details in sequence:
1. Business name
2. Business category (barber, salon, restaurant, etc.)
3. Services/menu and prices
4. Confirmation of services
5. Business hours
6. Address

### Step 3: Review and confirm
At important steps (like services), Shopfront sends a confirmation summary before publishing.

### Step 4: Website goes live
After onboarding completion, Shopfront generates and publishes the business page URL.

### What the customer gets immediately
- A mobile-first public page
- Structured service/price display
- Hours and notices sections
- Contact actions (call/text)
- Search/social metadata ready for sharing

## Part B: Regular Upkeep (Day-to-Day Use)

Owners send normal language messages. Examples:
- “Change haircut to $30”
- “Add beard shaping for $20”
- “Open till 8 on Fridays”
- “Closed next Monday”
- “Update address to 742 Evergreen Terrace”
- “Make this my main photo” (with image)

### Update flow customers experience
1. Owner sends a request
2. Shopfront classifies intent and extracts details
3. Shopfront asks for confirmation when a database change is needed
4. Owner replies yes/no
5. If yes, Shopfront updates data and rebuilds the page
6. Owner gets a completion message

### Expected turnaround
Most updates are reflected on the public page within seconds.

## Part C: Support and Issue Resolution

## AI-First Support Channel
When users need help, they can text support in plain language:
- “My page isn’t updating”
- “I can’t change hours”
- “Wrong photo is showing”
- “I need help with onboarding”

The AI support assistant will first:
1. Understand the issue
2. Run guided troubleshooting steps
3. Attempt safe, immediate fixes where possible
4. Confirm whether the issue is resolved

## Human Escalation (When AI Can’t Resolve)
If the AI cannot solve the issue with high confidence, it escalates to a human support agent.

### Escalation triggers
- Repeated failed attempts
- Potential billing/account issues
- Ambiguous data conflicts
- Suspected platform-side incident
- User explicitly asks for a human

### Human handoff details
The AI includes context for fast resolution:
- Business phone + shop ID
- Recent message history
- Detected intent and attempted actions
- Error details captured by monitoring

This prevents customers from repeating the same information.

## Customer FAQ (Homepage-Ready)

### Do I need technical skills?
No. You can run your website by texting updates in plain language.

### Can I update anytime?
Yes. Send updates whenever your business changes.

### What if I send unclear instructions?
Shopfront asks follow-up questions or confirmation before applying changes.

### What happens if something breaks?
AI support responds first and escalates to human support when needed.

### Can this work for my type of business?
Yes. Shopfront supports service businesses, food businesses, and general local businesses.
